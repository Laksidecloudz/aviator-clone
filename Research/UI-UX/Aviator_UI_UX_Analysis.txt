AVIATOR GAME

UI/UX Analysis Document

Source: Spribe Official Aviator Demo

Analysis Date: March 30, 2026

1. CURVE / FLIGHT PATH ANIMATION

Shape & Mathematical Behavior

The curve follows an EXPONENTIAL growth pattern, not linear. The
multiplier starts at 1.00x and accelerates rapidly as time progresses.
The curve appears to follow a formula similar to: multiplier = e^(kt)
where k is a growth constant.

OBSERVED BEHAVIOR: The curve starts relatively flat (gentle slope) at
1x-2x, then becomes progressively steeper. By 5x, the curve is climbing
at approximately 45 degrees; by 10x+, it approaches near-vertical
ascent.

Slope Progression at Key Milestones

  • 1x-1.99x: Very gentle slope, approximately 10-15 degrees

  • 2x-4.99x: Moderate slope, approximately 25-35 degrees

  • 5x-9.99x: Steep slope, approximately 45-60 degrees

  • 10x+: Very steep, approaching 70-80 degrees

  • 50x+: Near-vertical, almost 85+ degrees

Derivative Behavior Near Crash

CRITICAL OBSERVATION: The curve does NOT flatten before crash. The plane
maintains its exponential acceleration trajectory until the exact moment
of crash. There is no plateau or deceleration warning - the crash
happens instantaneously while the curve is still steeply ascending.

This creates the tension mechanic: players see the curve getting steeper
and steeper, knowing it could crash at any moment while the potential
reward increases exponentially.

Curve Visual Style

  • Line Width: Approximately 3-4 pixels thick

  • Color: Bright lime green (#86E96D) with glow effect

  • Glow: Soft outer glow in same green color, approximately 8-10px
  radius

  • Fill: Gradient fill under the curve from green to transparent

  • Anti-aliasing: Smooth, high-quality curve rendering

Time to Reach Milestones (Typical Mid-Range Crash)

  • 1x to 2x: Approximately 2-3 seconds

  • 2x to 5x: Approximately 3-4 seconds

  • 5x to 10x: Approximately 4-6 seconds

  • Total to 10x: Approximately 10-12 seconds from start

Note: These timings vary based on the provably fair crash point
generation. The video shows a 2.58x crash and an 11.18x crash.

2. PLANE SPRITE & ANIMATION

Plane Design Details

The plane is a stylized RED biplane/monoplane hybrid with the following
characteristics:

  • Fuselage: Sleek, streamlined red body with subtle gradient shading

  • Wings: Single main wing (monoplane style) with white/light gray tips

  • Tail: Classic vertical stabilizer with horizontal elevators

  • Propeller: Visible at front, animated with motion blur effect

  • Cockpit: Small, implied cockpit area

  • Markings: No visible text or numbers on the plane itself

Color Specification

  • Primary Red: Bright, saturated red (#E53935 or similar)

  • Wing Tips: White or very light gray

  • Propeller: Silver/gray with motion blur

  • Shading: Subtle darker red gradient on underside

Bob/Floating Animation

The plane exhibits a subtle vertical bobbing motion independent of the
curve movement:

  • Amplitude: Approximately 5-10 pixels up/down

  • Frequency: Approximately 2-3 complete cycles per second

  • Pattern: Sine wave oscillation, smooth and natural

  • Purpose: Adds life and dynamism to the sprite

Propeller Animation

  • Animation: Continuous rotation

  • Speed: Fast rotation, approximately 10-15 RPM visually

  • Visual: Motion blur circle effect, not distinct blades

  • Color: Silver/gray blur circle

Angle/Tilt Behavior

The plane rotates to follow the curve tangent:

  • At 1x: Nearly horizontal, slight upward angle (~10 degrees)

  • At 2x: Moderate climb angle (~25 degrees)

  • At 5x: Steep angle (~45 degrees)

  • At 10x+: Very steep angle (~60-70 degrees)

The rotation is smooth and follows the instantaneous slope of the curve,
creating the illusion that the plane is 'flying' along the path.

Position on Screen

  • Horizontal: Plane stays at approximately 60-70% from left edge

  • Vertical: Plane moves upward as multiplier increases

  • Camera: Follows the plane, panning up and right as curve ascends

  • Trail: Particle trail follows behind the plane

3. FLY-AWAY / CRASH ANIMATION

Crash Behavior

At the moment of crash, the following sequence occurs:

  • 1. Plane INSTANTLY disappears - no fade, no explosion

  • 2. The curve stops drawing immediately

  • 3. 'FLEW AWAY!' text appears above the final multiplier

  • 4. Final multiplier turns RED and remains displayed

  • 5. Background color shifts to darker purple/magenta

'FLEW AWAY!' Text Animation

  • Position: Center of game area, above the multiplier

  • Size: Large, approximately 48-64px font

  • Color: White text (#FFFFFF)

  • Font: Bold, sans-serif (likely system font)

  • Animation: Fade-in from 0% to 100% opacity

  • Duration: Text remains visible for approximately 3-5 seconds

  • Style: All caps, with exclamation mark

Final Multiplier Display

  • Position: Center, below 'FLEW AWAY!' text

  • Color Change: Transitions from white to BRIGHT RED

  • Size: Very large, dominant display (approximately 80-100px)

  • Decimals: Always shows 2 decimal places (e.g., '2.58x')

  • Duration: Remains visible until next round starts

Screen Effects

  • Flash: NO bright flash at crash

  • Shake: NO screen shake

  • Color Overlay: Background shifts to darker purple/magenta tone

  • Transition: Smooth fade to lobby state

Particle Effects

NO visible particle burst at crash point. The crash is a clean
disappearance. However, during flight:

  • Trail particles: Small white/yellow dots trailing behind plane

  • Particle behavior: Fade out over time, create motion streak

  • Density: Moderate, approximately 20-30 visible particles

4. MULTIPLIER DISPLAY

Position & Layout

  • Position: Center of game canvas

  • Anchor: Stays centered horizontally, moves up as curve climbs

  • Z-Index: Above curve, below plane

Size & Scale

  • Font Size: Approximately 15-20% of canvas height

  • At 1x: Large, readable size

  • At 10x+: Same size, no scaling with multiplier value

Color Behavior (State-Based)

  • 1.00x - 1.99x: White (#FFFFFF)

  • 2.00x - 9.99x: White (#FFFFFF) - no color change

  • 10.00x+: Purple/Magenta tint on background, text remains white

  • At Crash: Turns BRIGHT RED (#FF0000 or #E53935)

Font Specifications

  • Typeface: Sans-serif, bold weight

  • Style: Monospace-like numeric display

  • Weight: Bold (700)

  • Rendering: Anti-aliased, crisp edges

Animation Effects

  • Number Update: Smooth increment, no jitter

  • Decimal Animation: Hundredths place changes rapidly

  • Pulse: Subtle pulse/glow effect at higher multipliers

  • Scale: No scale animation, fixed size

Decimal Places

  • Format: Always 2 decimal places

  • Display: '1.00x', '2.58x', '11.18x'

  • Suffix: Lowercase 'x' immediately after numbers

  • Separator: Period (.) for decimal

5. BACKGROUND

Visual Style

The background features a dramatic SUNBURST/RADIAL RAY pattern emanating
from the bottom-left corner:

  • Pattern: Radiating rays like sunbeams or spotlight effect

  • Origin: Bottom-left corner of canvas

  • Direction: Rays extend toward top-right

  • Count: Approximately 12-16 distinct rays

Color Scheme

  • Base Color: Very dark blue/black (#0A0E17 or similar)

  • Ray Color: Gradient from dark blue to lighter blue/purple

  • Ray Brightness: Subtle, not overpowering

  • Edge Softness: Rays have soft, blurred edges

Dynamic Background Changes

The background changes based on game state:

  • Lobby/Waiting: Dark with subtle rays

  • Flight (1x-2x): Blue-tinted rays

  • Flight (2x-5x): Blue-purple gradient rays

  • Flight (5x+): Purple/magenta tinted rays

  • Crash: Dark purple/magenta dominant

Animation

  • Ray Movement: Subtle rotation/pulse (very slow)

  • Color Transition: Smooth gradient shifts during flight

  • Intensity: Rays become more visible as multiplier increases

Grid Lines

  • Presence: NO visible grid lines in the observed version

  • Alternative: The curve itself provides the only visual reference

Note: Some Aviator variants may have subtle grid lines, but the official
Spribe version observed uses the sunburst pattern exclusively.

8. GAME STATE TRANSITIONS

State Machine Overview

The game follows a clear finite state machine: LOBBY -> BETTING ->
FLYING -> CRASHED -> LOBBY

LOBBY State

  • Visual: 'UFC Aviator OFFICIAL PARTNERS' logo displayed

  • Background: Dark with subtle rays

  • Multiplier: Not displayed

  • Buttons: Green 'Bet' buttons active

  • Duration: Variable, typically 5-10 seconds between rounds

LOBBY -> BETTING Transition

  • Trigger: Timer countdown or manual bet placement

  • Visual Change: 'Cancel' button appears after bet

  • Text: 'Waiting for next round' appears

  • Animation: Smooth fade transition

BETTING State

  • Visual: Logo fades, plane prepares to appear

  • Countdown: Brief countdown before flight

  • Bet Status: 'Cancel' button shows bet is locked

  • Duration: Approximately 3-5 seconds

BETTING -> FLYING Transition

  • Trigger: Countdown reaches zero

  • Plane Appearance: Plane fades in at bottom-left

  • Curve Start: Curve begins drawing from (0,0)

  • Multiplier: '1.00x' appears and starts increasing

  • Button Change: 'Cancel' becomes 'Cash Out' (orange)

  • Animation: Smooth 0.5 second transition

FLYING State

  • Visual: Plane flying along curve

  • Multiplier: Continuously updating

  • Background: Color shifts with multiplier

  • Cash Out: Button shows real-time win amount

  • Duration: Variable, 2-30+ seconds depending on crash point

FLYING -> CRASHED Transition

  • Trigger: Crash point reached (provably fair)

  • Plane: Instantly disappears

  • Curve: Stops drawing immediately

  • Text: 'FLEW AWAY!' fades in

  • Multiplier: Turns red, stops updating

  • Duration: Instant crash, 3-5 seconds display

CRASHED -> LOBBY Transition

  • Trigger: After crash display timeout

  • Visual: Fade to UFC logo

  • Reset: All bets cleared, new round setup

  • Duration: Approximately 2-3 seconds

  • Next Round: Setup begins immediately
